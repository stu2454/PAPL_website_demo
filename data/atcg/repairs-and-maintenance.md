# Repairs and Maintenance
The NDIA provides funding in participants’ Capital budget for the repair and maintenance of AT items costing more than $1,500. Warranty repair (including Australian Consumer Law warranties: https://www.accc.gov.au/focus-areas/information-for/consumers-with-disability) of AT should be discussed with the supplier or manufacturer and is not a reasonable and necessary support. Low-cost repairs and maintenance (e.g., repairing a strap on a prosthesis) outside the warranty period should generally be paid using a low-cost AT Support Item (allowing the greatest flexibility). Alternatively, the participant could use their unallocated Capital (AT) budget where appropriate.
Higher cost repairs (>$1,500) are likely to be covered by a quotable Repairs and Maintenance Support Item included in the participant’s plan. The tables below contain repair codes against relevant AT categories.
To allow flexibility, most repair and maintenance funding is in the Capital budget, unallocated against any particular Support Item. Providers should use the relevant support item to claim participant agreed repairs to NDIS funded AT.
Most of these support items are not subject to price limits, and use the Notional Amount claiming approach. See page 12 for further information on how to claim for support items that are not subject to a price limit.

# Home Modifications (Support Category 06)
This support category includes both Home Modifications (HM) and Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA) supports. Additional information can be found in the NDIS Pricing Arrangements for Special Disability Accommodation: https://www.ndis.gov.au/providers/housing-and-living-supports-and-services/specialist-disability-accommodation/sda-pricing-and-payments#sda-price-guide.
In this guide, Home Modifications (HM) are changes to a home to improve a participant’s safety and independence. Home modifications include design and construction, as well as installation of fixtures or fittings and changes to structural and non-structural components of the home. Where participants are building a new house, they can access supports to help design and the cost difference to construct disability related features. Participants may also access supports to help them relocate to a new house, where their existing house requires extensive and is of high cost to modify. The Home Modifications Guidance for Builders and Designers: https://www.ndis.gov.au/providers/housing-and-living-supports-and-services/providing-home-modifications#complex-home-modification-chm-assessments provides further clarity on the diverse range of home modifications that the NDIS may consider reasonable and necessary.
Some simple low-cost home adaptation items (<$1,500) may be claimed as Assistive Technology under the “Assistive products for household tasks and access” category. They should not be separately claimed if they are part of a Home Modification support category (for example grab rails would be included in a Minor Bathroom modification). An Occupational Therapist can recommend simple home adaptations for a participant.
All Home Modification support needs must be assessed by a home modification assessor. A home modification assessor is an Occupational Therapist who is suitably qualified to assess, implement and review home modification supports.
Minor Home Modification (MHM) includes changes to a home that are relatively low risk, straightforward, non-structural, and generally cost less than $20,000 in total. The pre-determined budgets allocated to participants who require MHM supports are based on where the participant lives and current market costs that are updated periodically. Further detail is published in the Operational Guideline - Home Modifications. Providers should use the appropriate MHM code for the works undertaken, plus the MHM Practical Completion code (equivalent to 10% of the works budget) after the completion of the works. Note: Budgets provided are in line with the expectation that GST would not apply (see Goods and Services Tax (GST)).
Complex Home Modifications (CHM) include changes to the home that are often structural changes and custom-built, have a higher risk of issues occurring, often involve multiple areas of the home and generally cost more than $20,000 in total. CHM usually need building approvals or permissions, and certification of work. A CHM assessment must be undertaken by home modification assessor. Funding for a building construction practitioner (BCP) will be included to work with the participant and home modification assessor to determine the most effective way to install the recommended CHM. The BCP can also provide a cost estimate for the NDIA to review the CHM, otherwise 2 builder’s quotes are required.
An approved CHM will be described against the specific home modification support item codes shown below.
Capital budget funding will be provided for modifications that are reasonable and necessary for the participant. A participant is free to choose a more expensive option at their own expense, where the more expensive option is not reasonable and necessary. For example, where a home modification has been approved for a participant, but the participant would like cosmetic or personalised fittings that are above that considered reasonable and necessary, the NDIA will cover the cost of reasonable and necessary modifications, and the participant will pay the additional costs.
## Home modifications
This area incorporates design and subsequent changes to the participant’s home. Home modification design and construction includes installation of equipment or changes to building structures, fixture or fittings to enable participants to live more independently and safely at home.
Standard furniture, fixtures and fittings are considered an NDIS support when recommended and included as part of a home modification. Standard furniture, fixtures and fittings can include:
- Tapware, handheld shower hose, toilet roll holder and towel rail
- Vanity and toilet suites
- Cabinetry in the kitchen and laundry
- Appliances, such as stovetops and portable ovens
- Air conditioner
- Replacement flooring, such as tiles and vinyl planking
- Door hardware, light switches and skirting and framing
